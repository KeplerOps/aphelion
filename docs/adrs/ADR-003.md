# ADR-003: Deliver a Strong Single-Node Product Before Distributed HA

- **Status**: Accepted
- **Date**: 2026-04-05
- **Superseded by**: None

## Context

Building everything at once (new DB, server, forked kernel, distributed HA, enterprise hardening) creates compounding risk.

## Decision

Single-node system first. In scope: durability, crash recovery, security, backup, restore, observability, operational controls. Out of scope: distributed consensus, multi-node replication, transparent failover, distributed transactions, cross-node query execution.

## Consequences

**Positive:**
- Reduces initial surface area to a tractable scope.
- Forces correctness and hardening on a system that can be fully reasoned about.

**Negative:**
- No built-in HA for initial releases.

**Risks:**
- Boundaries between single-node and future distributed work may not be preserved cleanly.
- Scope creep pulls distributed concerns into the initial product.
